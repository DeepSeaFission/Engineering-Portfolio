# 1U CubeSat Power Supply - UCubeSat Collegiate Club Activity
Generated as Team Lead for the Photovoltaics and Power Distribution Team

This project was my attempt to produce a reliable 1U CubeSat Power Supply powered by two Li-Po 18650 batteries. 

![Final PCB Render](Images/PCB%20Screenshot.png)

## Mk 1

This PCB went through several iterations before reaching this final stage. Initially, when the UCubeSat club was just getting started and our requirements were ill-defined, I began with a dual 12V/5V power supply just to have something for the team to work on and to generate interest. That design included a boost converter to generate the 12V, since the combined voltage of the 18650 batteries was only around 8.4V at max charge. 

## Mk 2

After further design refining, we determined only a 5V rail was necessary. Intead of a buck converter that would need to operate at high efficiency across the full range of voltage the batteries would produce in series, I opted for a design relying on voltage following OpAmps following a stable 5V positive terminal voltage generated by a zener diode. The zener diode branch draws low current thanks to high resistance resistors in series with the diode. 

Unfortunately, my first iteration of this design had a few flaws. I wired the Opamp outputs together with the intention of having a combined source of power. When simulated in LTspice, this seemed to work well. However, in practice, this led to the feedback loops for each OpAmp fighting, leading to rapid overheating and failure. I also was attempting to solder USB-C ports to the PCB, which is extremely difficult to do by hand.

## Mk 3

This led to my final iteration. 

![Final Schematic](Images/Schematic%20Screenshot.png)

Each OpAmp was given a dedicated output line with an RLC filter, supplying 1 Amp at 5V. The connectors were removed with the intention that any power taps would just be soldered directly to the board using vias. This iteration is capable of powering every load we have planned for several hours. In the future, we believe it will require minimal modification to work with a solar power supply for recharging.
